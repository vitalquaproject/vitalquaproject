ETAP Kenya — How it works

We'll walk you through the seven-stage journey that turns raw, turbid water into crystal-clear drinking water for a Kenyan school — powered entirely by the sun, with zero operating cost. Scroll to follow the drop along the way.

1

Night Mode

The system is completely dormant. With no energy, the flow is halted. This is the baseline of our OpEx 0 facility in Kenya, quietly waiting for the first sun rays to initiate the purification cycle.

2

Solar Pumping

Solar energy hits the panels and activates the submerged pump. A steady flow of raw, turbid water begins its journey from the source, powered entirely by a 100% off-grid, zero-carbon solution.

3

Buffer Tank

The 6000L tank acts as a hydraulic buffer, ensuring a perfectly stabilized flow of 208 L/h. Integrated LoRaWAN telemetry monitors the levels in real-time, autonomously managing the intake pump.

4

Cascade Aeration

Gravity forces the water down steps, maximizing the air-water interface. This natural process effectively strips away foul gases like H2S and oxidizes dissolved Iron into solid, filterable particles.

5

Settling Tank

By drastically slowing the water velocity to just 0.08 m/h, gravity takes over. Over 60% of the heavy mud and flocs sink and remain safely trapped on the specially designed sloped floor.

6

Roughing Filter (URF)

The water gently rises through three specific layers of gravel. Fine clay particles stick to the rough stones through physical adsorption, leaving the water visually clear and protecting downstream filters.

7

Slow Sand Filter

This is the biological engine of the system. A living, natural ecosystem called the Schmutzdecke develops on the sand bed, actively predating on dangerous bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

8

Biochar Polishing

The final touch of chemical innovation. Locally sourced biochar acts as a powerful sponge, capturing any remaining trace organics and odors, and delivering fresh, crystal-clear water to the school.
